That license is **free** and covers essentially every non-commercial use:

- personal use, hobby projects, and self-study
- academic and scientific research
- teaching and coursework
- use by universities, non-profits, public research, and government

**You do not need to pay or ask for anything to use Ariadne for those purposes.**
Use it, fork it, build on it, publish results from it. We only ask that you keep
the copyright/notice intact (the license requires it).

## When you need a commercial license

You need a separate, paid commercial license if you use Ariadne — in whole or in
part, modified or unmodified — for **commercial advantage**, including:

- inside a product or service you sell, host, or monetize
- internal use at a for-profit company as part of doing business (including a
  for-profit subsidiary, affiliate, or arm of a non-profit)
- using it to build, train, evaluate, or improve a commercial product, service,
  dataset, or AI/ML model
- consulting or paid analysis delivered to clients
- any use whose purpose is commercial gain

Being a non-profit does not by itself grant commercial rights: what matters is the
*purpose* of the use. A non-profit doing genuine non-commercial research is free; a
for-profit company (or the for-profit arm of one) using Ariadne for commercial
advantage needs a license.

If you are not sure which side of the line you fall on, ask — we would rather have
a quick conversation than leave you guessing.
